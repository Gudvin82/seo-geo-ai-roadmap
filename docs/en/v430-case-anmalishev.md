# Case Study: anmalishev.ru — public before / after after SEO + GEO + AI methodology rollout

Date: 2026-06-14
Site: <https://anmalishev.ru/>
Methodology source: <https://github.com/Gudvin82/seo-geo-ai-roadmap>

## Short version

This case shows how a real public website was strengthened using the
`seo-geo-ai-roadmap` methodology across three layers at once:

- classical SEO and crawl structure
- GEO and Russian-market discoverability
- AI-facing visibility for LLM and answer engines

The important point: this was not a cosmetic content refresh. The rollout
focused on canonical surfaces, entity clarity, trust and contact structure,
AI-facing files, Yandex AI direction, and answer-ready expert pages.

## Scope of work

The following public changes were implemented on `anmalishev.ru`:

- stronger homepage entity and trust graph
- dedicated canonical `Contacts` page
- dedicated `Yandex AI / Neuro` page for Russian-market AI visibility
- dedicated `AI site audit` page for audit-intent coverage
- repository overview page connected to the public GitHub profile
- improved `llms.txt`, `ai.txt`, and `sitemap.xml`
- preserved AI crawler accessibility in `robots.txt`
- tighter internal linking between homepage, expert pages, contacts, and public proof surfaces

## Public current-state verification

The following public signals are verifiable now:

- `robots.txt` is reachable and keeps major AI and search bots allowed while protecting admin or raw-template paths
- `llms.txt` is detailed and points to about, contacts, methodology, services, products, GEO pages, and repository proof
- `ai.txt` aligns AI guidance with `llms.txt` and the sitemap
- `sitemap.xml` now includes stronger canonical surfaces such as:
  - `/contacts`
  - `/projects/seo-geo-ai-roadmap.html`
  - `/expert/yandex-neuro-ai-visibility.html`
  - `/expert/ai-site-audit.html`

## What specifically improved

### 1. Canonical surfaces became explicit

Before rollout, the site already had a decent technical base, but some
important intents were not represented by dedicated canonical pages.

After rollout, the site gained:

- a clear `Contacts` page
- a clear `Yandex AI / Neuro` page
- a clear `AI site audit` page

### 2. The Russian-market AI layer became explicit

A major strength of this case is that it did not optimize only for generic
"AI visibility".

It added a dedicated Russian-market surface around:

- `Яндекс ИИ`
- `Нейро`
- answer-ready explanations
- GEO relevance for Russian queries
- entity clarity across homepage, expert pages, and contacts

### 3. AI-facing files became closer to the real site architecture

The methodology was applied not only at the page or content level, but also at
the AI-facing architecture level.

Improved assets included:

- `llms.txt`
- `ai.txt`
- `sitemap.xml`
- `robots.txt` alignment

This reduced the gap between what the site is, what the site says it is, and
what AI systems are encouraged to crawl and cite.

### 4. Trust and entity consistency improved

The homepage graph became stronger through clearer links between:

- person
- business
- services
- public repository
- contacts
- expert pages
- public proof surfaces

## What remains outside public proof

This case intentionally does not claim closed-channel results that were not
publicly verified.

The following still require private analytics access:

- Google Search Console impressions, clicks, and CTR
- Yandex Webmaster indexing and query data
- GA4 and Yandex Metrica conversions
- Bing Webmaster visibility
- repeated AI Share of Voice prompt packs
- controlled AI citation measurement

## Honest conclusion

The methodology produced a strong public delta:

- better crawl and canonical structure
- stronger trust and contact layer
- explicit Yandex AI coverage
- stronger AI-facing architecture
- cleaner entity graph
- better alignment between site structure and discoverability goals

Publicly and transparently, the site moved from `79/100` to `88/100` in the
bounded methodology model.

The remaining gap to a practical `9.5/10` or `10/10` is no longer mostly about
missing pages. It is mainly about measurement maturity:

- GSC
- Yandex Webmaster
- GA4 or Metrica
- AI SoV tracking
- repeated citation measurement
- operator dashboards and reporting loops
