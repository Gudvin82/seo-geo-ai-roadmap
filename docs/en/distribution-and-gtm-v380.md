# Distribution and GTM v3.8.0

`v3.8.0` makes the repository easier to adopt, easier to package, and easier to
sell.

## The commercial promise

This repository is strongest when sold or adopted as:

- a self-hosted discoverability operating system
- an audit and re-measurement workflow
- a client delivery and evidence engine
- an AI-agent-friendly implementation base

## Use-case packs

- Agency: one workspace per client, recurring reporting, graph walkthroughs,
  fix packs, and bilingual exports
- Founder: one self-hosted stack, one site, periodic audits, proof-first
  improvement loop
- In-house: shared truth center, provider-backed SoV checks, and recurring
  regressions monitoring
- Consultant: pre-sales quick audit, graph explanation, then scoped backlog

## Sales-ready deliverables

- executive summary
- fix pack
- graph JSON snapshot
- client delivery pack
- weekly or monthly operations update

## Canonical sequence

1. research with `/geo quick` or `/geo audit`
2. explain with `/geo graph`
3. prioritize with `/geo report`
4. improve with content, schema, technical, and trust work
5. re-measure with `/geo compare`

## Open-source vs commercial boundary

Open-source core:

- methodology
- self-hosted product
- scripts
- docs
- templates
- graph UX

Commercial or service layer:

- your hosting
- your operators
- your client service
- your custom integrations
- your delivery motion

The repository gives the engine. Commercial outcomes still require execution.
