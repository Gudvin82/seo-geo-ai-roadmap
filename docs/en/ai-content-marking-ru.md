# RU AI-Content Marking Guidance

This is practical RU-market compliance guidance, not legal advice.

Use it when a site operates in Russian-speaking markets and AI-assisted content
creates trust, expert-authority, or factual-risk questions.

## When disclosure is prudent

- expert or trust-heavy pages
- legal, finance, health, or compliance-adjacent content
- comparison or advisory content with non-trivial factual risk
- cases where AI assistance should not be mistaken for unreviewed expert authorship

## Safe framing

- disclose AI assistance honestly
- state that human review happened if it really happened
- avoid implying expert certainty where only draft assistance existed

## Why this matters for GEO

- trust signals affect whether content is believable and citable
- factual consistency matters more than decorative AI enthusiasm
- honest disclosure can reduce reputational and legal risk
